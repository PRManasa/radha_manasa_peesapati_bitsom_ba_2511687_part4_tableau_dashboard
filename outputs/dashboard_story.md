# Dashboard Story
**For:** Leadership Team  
**From:** Radha Manasa Peesapati | **Date:** June 2026

---

## Executive Summary
The dashboard covers 4,200 orders across 4 regions, 3 categories, and 3 customer segments. Overall the business is performing well with consistent ~15% profit margins across all regions and segments. Two areas need attention: high-discount orders are generating losses, and Furniture returns are significantly higher than other categories.

---

## What Is Performing Well

Technology is the dominant profit driver by a large margin. Copiers, Phones, and Accessories together account for most of the total profit despite being just one of three categories.

South region leads in sales volume at ₹6.47 Cr. Same Day and First Class shipping are reliable with average delivery under 2 days. Profit margins are consistent across all three customer segments — no segment is pulling down overall profitability.

---

## What Is Underperforming

Furniture contributes relatively little profit despite generating sales. Its return rate of 7.67% is more than double the other categories, adding reverse logistics costs on top of low margins.

Office Supplies profit is thin — high order volumes but low contribution. Standard Class shipping averages 4.71 days which may be affecting satisfaction for customers expecting faster delivery.

---

## Risks Visible

High discounts are causing losses. Orders with discounts above 25% have average negative margins in Furniture. Without a discount cap, individual large orders can generate losses of nearly ₹18,000 per order.

Furniture return rate is elevated across all three customer segments, with Home Office showing the highest rate. This suggests a product or delivery quality issue rather than a segment-specific behaviour.

---

## Opportunities Visible

South region's consistent outperformance suggests something replicable — whether it is campaign mix, product preference, or regional demand patterns. Understanding this could unlock growth in East and West.

Shifting customers from Standard Class to First Class could improve satisfaction without a major cost increase, based on the delivery day data visible in the dashboard.

---

## Recommended Actions

1. Introduce a discount cap — require approval for discounts above 20%
2. Review Furniture product quality and packaging to reduce the 7.67% return rate
3. Analyse South region's drivers and apply learnings to East and West
4. Promote First Class shipping as a value option over Standard Class
5. Review Standard Class SLA performance — 4.71 days average may be a satisfaction risk

---

## Limitations
- Customer rating has 32 missing values — satisfaction analysis is approximate
- Campaign channel has 24 missing records — attribution analysis may undercount some channels
- Return reasons are not captured — root cause analysis on Furniture returns is limited to pattern observation
- Data covers a fixed time window — longer date range needed for seasonality analysis

---

## Suggested Next Analysis
- Campaign channel ROI — which channels drive the highest value orders
- Furniture sub-category deep dive — which products drive the 7.67% return rate
- Cohort analysis — do customers acquired through different channels have different lifetime values
